Why We Do It

Because Every Life Matters

In The Gambia, hundreds of people are buried alive every year. Many deaths are caused by ignorance, inadequate first aid, and incompetent handling of casualties. CLA was born to change this reality.

Preventable Deaths
Everyday road, sea, industrial and domestic accidents claim lives that proper First Aid training could have saved.
Wrongful Death Announcements
In Muslim communities, where the deceased must be buried within 24 hours, imams and elders untrained in medicine may mistake unconsciousness for death — leading to people being buried alive.
Our Mission
We train, educate, and equip communities across The Gambia to make the right decisions when it matters most — and to make our nation a safer, better place.
Our History

Two Decades of
Caring for Life

Caring for Life Association (CLA) is a registered voluntary relief charity in the Gambia, founded in 2004 — one of the strongest pioneers in Health & Safety training and services, and entirely Gambian-led.

Our qualified, licensed tutors, nurses, lifeguards and driving instructors hold certifications from recognized institutions both in The Gambia and abroad. We've delivered training and services across all sectors of society — for institutions and individuals alike.

Driven by the conviction that we can have a safer world, and that each person has the power to protect and save lives, we strive to reach out to everyone — spurred on by our vision of strong, healthy communities.

2004
CLA registered as a voluntary charity in The Gambia. Founded in Bundung as the nation's leading first aid organization.
2005
Joined the ActionAid Global campaign to fight HIV/AIDS. Began mass awareness campaigns in partnership with CSOs and community groups.
2007
Established the CLA Research Unit — one of the Gambia's major public investments in health research for developing countries.
2011
Documented 176 work-related accident claims via NASSIT, strengthening the case for mandatory workplace First Aid training across the country.
Today
Continuing to serve The Gambia with First Aid training, ambulance services, HIV prevention, disaster response, and youth programmes.

HIV/AIDS Programme

Fighting the Pandemic Together

Since 2005, CLA has partnered with ActionAid, NGOs, women's groups, and communities across The Gambia to combat HIV/AIDS through education, advocacy, and solidarity.

✅ Major Achievements

High-level awareness about HIV/AIDS raised across The Gambia through mass sensitisation campaigns.

Supported the development of the first-ever five-year HIV/AIDS strategy for NAYCO (National Association of Youths and Children's Organization).

Assisted Children Against HIV/AIDS (CAA) establish a peer learning and sharing centre.

10 illiterate women trained in adult literacy to improve their understanding of treatment literacy.

Two PLHAs sponsored for a two-week advocacy training study tour in Uganda.

Created a national network of Women Living with HIV/AIDS linked to sub-regional bodies.

Five Thematic Goals

People living with HIV will increasingly claim and exercise their rights, including access to comprehensive treatment, care and social security.
Women and girls' vulnerability to HIV infection will be measurably reduced through the exercise of their rights.
All vulnerable persons will have the information and skills to protect themselves from HIV infection.
PLHAs and citizens will have the organizations needed to create sustainable responses to HIV/AIDS.
States and institutions will be accountable to their citizens, particularly regarding the rights of people living with HIV/AIDS.

How We Do It

Our Approach

We reach people in new and exciting ways — building networks, reinforcing partnerships, and winning public and legislative support for First Aid across The Gambia.

01

Advocacy & Awareness

Event promotion, advocacy programmes, lobbying with authorities and publishing guidelines to revalue the image of First Aid in the Gambia.

02

Training & Quality

Harmonizing courses and materials, training First Aid management, and maintaining quality assurance across all programmes.

03

Partnerships

Building networks across sectors — NGOs, CSOs, government departments, women's groups and international organizations.

04

Research & Development

Increasing market and technical research for product development, and conducting field research to improve health outcomes.

05

Resource Mobilisation

Local income generation through commercial training, membership drives, ambulance services and defensive driving instruction.

06

Volunteer Power

Motivated, trained volunteers — Juniors (5–10), Cadets (10–17), and adults 18+ — are the backbone of everything we do.
